Learn about InteroHR

Understand interoception in practice.

InteroHR is built around a simple idea: people can get better at noticing internal signals and responding to them more skillfully. The app turns that idea into a practical training experience using heart-rate awareness and recovery exercises.

Instead of only showing you data, InteroHR asks you to pause, estimate, reflect, and then compare what you sensed with what your connected device reports.

What InteroHR helps you practice

The app focuses on awareness first, then regulation. You practice noticing what is happening in your body, then experiment with ways to respond more effectively.

Estimate

Feel before checking

Pause and estimate your heart rate before seeing the device value. This supports body awareness rather than passive number-watching.

Reflect

Notice context

Think about what may be shaping your internal state, such as stress, movement, focus, rest, or recovery.

Regulate

Practice calming

Use guided recovery exercises to explore what helps your body settle more effectively in the moment.

How the training works

InteroHR keeps the flow simple so it can become a repeatable daily practice rather than a complicated data task.

Pulse Check

Awareness practice

You estimate your heart rate before revealing the device reading. Over time, this can help sharpen your sense of how your body feels under different conditions.

Recovery Challenge

Regulation practice

You work on bringing your heart rate down using breathing, attention, and other calming strategies while the app tracks the result.

What you need to use it

InteroHR is designed to work with compatible Bluetooth heart-rate devices that can send live heart-rate data to your phone.

Hardware

Bluetooth heart-rate input

A compatible Bluetooth heart-rate strap or wearable is needed for the core training flow. This allows the app to compare your estimate with a live reading.

Important

Wellness use only

InteroHR is intended for wellness and self-awareness training. It is not a medical device and should not be used for diagnosis, treatment, or medical decisions.

Why this kind of practice matters

Interoception influences how we notice stress, effort, fatigue, calm, and readiness. Training that awareness may help you respond more intentionally in daily life.

Everyday skill

Better noticing

Regular check-ins can help you catch internal changes earlier, whether you are resting, working, exercising, or recovering.

Applied skill

More grounded responses

Recovery practice lets you test calming strategies and learn which ones help your body respond best.
